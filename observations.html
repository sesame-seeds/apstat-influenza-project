I separated each response into two different groups: Group Alpha (vaccinated) and Group Beta (unvaccinated). I created box-and-whiskers plots for each group to represent the center and spread of the data. Since the data was skewed to the right, I decided to apply a square root transformation to the data to make it approximately normal. A cube root would have also been well, but I ultimately stuck with square root transformations to test for significance.

Conclusions and Thoughts

In conclusion, there was not any significant statistical evidence to prove neither questions to be true. Although the p-value observed in Question 2 was relatively closer, it still failed to fall below a significance level of 0.05.

What can be improved on?

One major flaw in my project was my experiment design. Vaccine research impliments more rigorous experimental design and often relies on hands-on collected data rather than data from surveys.

If I were to ever repeat this project again, I would definitely improve on my design by collecting data in a more concrete way instead of relying on estimations.
